Hedonic Pricing Model
Featured Quantitative Project

Built a regression-based hedonic pricing model using NYC housing data to isolate how environmental amenities and property characteristics influenced residential property values.

The hardest part was recognizing everything the model could not fully capture: buyer psychology, neighborhood perception, school quality, future development expectations, and local dynamics.

Carbon Offsets & Environmental Markets
Environmental Economics

Studied carbon offset markets, carbon credits, incentive design, and how environmental markets attempt to turn sustainability goals into measurable economic behavior.

My paper argues that carbon offsets can become a form of greenwashing when weak oversight, poor accountability, and unequal land control allow companies to claim climate progress without true decarbonization.

Healthcare Investment Research
Healthcare Technology

One of the most interesting healthcare opportunities I evaluated involved a physician-owned healthcare business in California. During the diligence process, I quickly learned how dependent the company was on its founder, who also served as the primary physician and maintained many of the patient relationships within the community. That experience introduced me to concepts such as founder transition planning, customer concentration risk, succession planning, patient retention, and operational continuity. It reinforced how healthcare investing often requires understanding people and operations just as much as financial performance.
