Featured Quantitative Project

Hedonic Pricing Model: What Is a Park Actually Worth?

I wanted to understand something simple: if people say green space improves quality of life, does that value show up in actual housing prices?

Method

Built a regression-based hedonic pricing model using NYC housing data to isolate how environmental amenities and property characteristics influenced residential property values.

Regression AnalysisEconometricsForecastingReal Estate

How I Approached the Error Term

The hardest part was not running the regression. The harder part was recognizing everything the model could not fully capture: buyer psychology, neighborhood perception, school quality, future development expectations, and local dynamics.

Those unobserved factors live in the error term. My goal was not to pretend the model captured everything. My goal was to reduce bias by choosing economically meaningful variables and checking whether the results made sense in the real world.

Cool takeaway: markets often place real value on things people assume are intangible. Green space is not just nice to have — it can become part of how a market prices quality of life.
